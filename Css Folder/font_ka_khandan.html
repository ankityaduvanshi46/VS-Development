The Bakwas Of My Friend

The Bakwas of My Friend They say that true friendship is shiny and rare, Built on deep secrets and mutual care. But our bond is different, it’s built on a whim, And a shared understanding our chances are slim. If danger approaches, I know where you stand: Right behind me, with a phone in your hand. Not calling for help or a medic to see, But filming my doom for a funny IG. Your memory's selective, your logic is flawed, Your text-back schedule is completely a fraud. You'll vanish for days, leaving chats on "un-read," Then send me a meme from your afternoon bed. We go out to eat and you promise to pay, Then "lose your wallet" the very same day. You order the salad but eat all my fries, While staring me down with your innocent eyes. You're terrible at directions, your driving's a fright, You swear that you’re coming, then sleep through the night. You tell me your secrets, which I quickly keep, Mainly because they are weirding me out while I sleep.
