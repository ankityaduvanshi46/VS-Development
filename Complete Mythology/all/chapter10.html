Bhagavad Gita - Chapter 10: Vibhuti Yoga

Bhagavad Gita - Chapter 10: Vibhuti Yoga

The Yoga of Divine Glories

Chapter Insights

Introduction

Chapter 10 of the Bhagavad Gita, known as Vibhuti Yoga or the Yoga of Divine Glories, focuses on Lord Krishna’s divine manifestations and opulences. Krishna reveals himself as the source of all creation and describes his divine qualities and manifestations in the universe, from natural phenomena to eminent beings. This chapter inspires awe and devotion, encouraging Arjuna and devotees to recognize the divine presence in all aspects of existence, fostering a deeper connection through Bhakti Yoga.

By illustrating his universal presence, Chapter 10 offers a profound vision for spiritual seekers, relevant for cultivating reverence and purpose in modern life.

Historical and Philosophical Context

Rooted in the Mahabharata, Chapter 10 builds on Upanishadic teachings about the divine’s omnipresence, blending Bhakti Yoga with Jnana Yoga. Krishna’s descriptions of his vibhutis (divine glories) in verses like 10.21-39 align with Vedic cosmology and Advaita Vedanta’s concept of Brahman as all-pervading. Set in the Kurukshetra war, this chapter addresses Arjuna’s need for inspiration, offering a divine perspective to strengthen his resolve.

Key Themes

  • Divine Omnipresence: Krishna is the essence of all creation, manifesting in its finest aspects.
  • Devotion Through Awe: Recognizing Krishna’s glories inspires devotion and surrender.
  • Universal Source: Krishna is the origin of all beings, qualities, and phenomena.
  • Spiritual Vision: Seeing the divine in everything transforms perception and deepens faith.
  • Divine Inspiration: Krishna’s revelations empower devotees to act with divine connection.
  • Inclusivity of Bhakti: Devotion to Krishna’s glories is accessible to all seekers.

Psychological Insights

Chapter 10’s focus on divine glories (10.21-39) fosters a sense of wonder, reducing ego-centric thinking and promoting humility. Recognizing the divine in everyday life (10.41) enhances mindfulness, aligning with psychological practices for gratitude and presence. Krishna’s assurance of his universal presence (10.40) provides comfort, reducing existential anxiety in uncertain times.

Cross-Cultural Relevance

The chapter’s concept of a divine essence in all creation resonates with monotheistic traditions like Christianity and Islam, where God is seen as omnipresent. The emphasis on awe and devotion parallels mystical practices in Sufism and Christian mysticism, while the universal vision aligns with pantheistic philosophies, making Chapter 10 globally relevant.

Philosophical Debates

Chapter 10 raises questions about the nature of the divine—personal versus impersonal—and the role of devotion in perceiving divine manifestations. Krishna’s vibhutis (10.21-39) spark discussions on whether the divine is immanent or transcendent, enriching metaphysical discourse on unity and diversity in creation.

Relevance Today

In 2025, Chapter 10’s teachings inspire awe in a world often dominated by materialism and division. Recognizing the divine in nature and humanity fosters environmental consciousness and compassion, addressing global challenges. The chapter’s vision of divine glories encourages ethical living and spiritual connection in a fast-paced, digital age.

Commentators’ Perspectives

Adi Shankaracharya (8th Century)

Shankaracharya interprets Chapter 10 as illustrating Brahman’s manifestations, with devotion as a path to non-dual realization. He views verses like 10.20 as pointing to the all-pervading divine.

Ramanujacharya (11th Century)

Ramanujacharya sees Chapter 10 as emphasizing devotion to Krishna as the Supreme Person, with his vibhutis (10.21-39) inspiring Bhakti in Vishishtadvaita.

Mahatma Gandhi (20th Century)

Gandhi drew on Chapter 10’s teachings to see the divine in all, applying this vision to his philosophy of universal love and non-violence during India’s freedom struggle.

Ethical Dilemmas and Resolutions

Arjuna’s Conflict

Arjuna seeks strength to face his duties in the war. Krishna resolves this by revealing his divine glories (10.21-39), inspiring awe and confidence, enabling Arjuna to act with divine purpose.

Modern Applications

Chapter 10 applies to dilemmas like finding meaning in routine life. For instance, recognizing the divine in work or relationships can inspire ethical decisions and a sense of higher purpose.

Meditation and Practice

Bhakti Yoga Practice

Chapter 10’s focus on divine glories (10.21-39) supports practices like chanting Krishna’s names or reflecting on his manifestations in nature, cultivating devotion.

Jnana Yoga Practice

Contemplate Krishna’s presence in all creation (10.20) through self-inquiry, recognizing the divine in everyday life to transcend material limitations.

Cultural Impact

Literature and Arts

Chapter 10 has inspired Indian art, with Krishna’s vibhutis depicted in paintings, sculptures, and classical dance. Its themes influence modern devotional music and literature.

Global Influence

The chapter’s vision of divine presence has shaped global spiritual movements, including ISKCON, and influenced interfaith dialogues on the divine in creation, promoting universal reverence.

Shlokas

Below is a selection of 20 key shlokas from Chapter 10, presented in Sanskrit with Hindi and English translations, accompanied by commentaries to highlight their significance.

Shloka 10.1

श्री भगवानुवाच
भूय एव महाबाहो शृणु मे परमं वचः।
यत्तेऽहं प्रीयमाणाय वक्ष्यामि हितकाम्यया।।

श्री भगवान बोले: हे महाबाहो! पुनः मेरे परम वचन सुन। मैं तुझ प्रिय के हित की इच्छा से यह कहूँगा।

The Blessed Lord said: Again, O mighty-armed, listen to My supreme word, which I shall speak to you, who are dear, for your benefit.

Commentary: Krishna introduces his divine teachings with affection, setting the stage for revealing his glories.

Shloka 10.2

न मे विदुः सुरगणाः प्रभवं न महर्षयः।
अहमादिर्हि देवानां महर्षीणां च सर्वशः।।

न देवता मेरे प्रभव को जानते हैं, न महर्षि। मैं सभी प्रकार से देवताओं और महर्षियों का आदि हूँ।

Neither the gods nor the great sages know My origin, for I am the source of the gods and sages in every way.

Commentary: Krishna asserts his supreme origin, inspiring awe and devotion as the source of all.

Shloka 10.3

यो मामजमजं विद्धि लोकानामीश्वरं च यः।
स सर्वं संसारमुक्तः सर्वपापैः प्रमुच्यति।।

जो मुझे अजन्मा और विश्व का ईश्वर जानता है, वह संसार से मुक्त होकर सभी पापों से मुक्त हो जाता है।

One who knows Me as unborn and the Lord of the worlds is undeluded among men and freed from all sins.

Commentary: Krishna highlights the liberating power of recognizing his divine nature, encouraging spiritual insight.

Shloka 10.4

बुद्धिर्ज्ञानमसंमोहः क्षमा सत्यं दमः शमः।
सुखं दुःखं भवोऽभावो भयं चाभयमेव च।।

बुद्धि, ज्ञान, असम्मोह, क्षमा, सत्य, दम, शम, सुख, दुख, भव, अभाव, भय और अभय भी मुझसे हैं।

Intellect, knowledge, clarity, forgiveness, truth, self-control, calmness, pleasure, pain, existence, non-existence, fear, and fearlessness are from Me.

Commentary: Krishna identifies himself as the source of all qualities, fostering recognition of the divine in human attributes.

Shloka 10.5

अहिंसा समता तुष्टिस्तपो दानं यशोऽयशः।
भवन्ति भावा भूतानां मत्त एव पृथग्विधाः।।

अहिंसा, समता, तुष्टि, तप, दान, यश और अपयश—ये प्राणियों के विभिन्न भाव मुझसे ही उत्पन्न होते हैं।

Non-violence, equanimity, contentment, austerity, charity, fame, and infamy—these various states of beings arise from Me alone.

Commentary: Krishna emphasizes his role as the origin of all states, encouraging devotees to see the divine in diversity.

Shloka 10.6

महर्षयः सप्त पूर्वे चत्वारो मनवस्तथा।
मद्भावा मानसा जाता येषां लोक इमाः प्रजाः।।

सात महर्षि, चार पूर्वज मनु, मेरे भाव से मानसिक रूप से उत्पन्न हुए, जिनसे इस लोक की प्रजा उत्पन्न हुई।

The seven great sages and the four ancient Manus, born of My mind, are imbued with My nature, from whom all beings in this world descend.

Commentary: Krishna traces the origin of creation to his divine will, reinforcing his supreme role.

Shloka 10.7

एतां विभूतिं योगं च मम यो वेत्ति तत्त्वतः।
सोऽविकम्पेन योगेन युज्यते नात्र संशयः।।

जो मेरी इस विभूति और योग को तत्त्वतः जानता है, वह अविचल योग से युक्त होता है, इसमें कोई संदेह नहीं।

One who truly knows My glories and divine Yoga is united through unwavering Yoga; there is no doubt about this.

Commentary: Krishna assures that understanding his divine nature leads to steadfast devotion and liberation.

Shloka 10.8

अहं सर्वस्य प्रभवो मत्तः सर्वं प्रवर्तति।
इति मत्वा भजन्ते मां बुधा भावसमन्विताः।।

मैं सबका प्रभव हूँ, मुझसे ही सब कुछ प्रवृत्त होता है। यह समझकर बुद्धिमान लोग भाव के साथ मुझे भजते हैं।

I am the source of all; from Me everything proceeds. Understanding this, the wise worship Me with devotion.

Commentary: Krishna’s role as the universal source inspires devoted worship among the wise.

Shloka 10.9

मच्चित्ता मद्गतप्राणा बोधयन्तः परस्परम्।
कथयन्तश्च मां नित्यं तुष्यन्ति च रमन्ति च।।

मेरे चित्त वाले, मेरे में प्राण अर्पित करने वाले, एक-दूसरे को बोध कराते और मेरी कथा कहते हुए, वे सदा संतुष्ट और आनंदित रहते हैं।

With minds fixed on Me, lives surrendered to Me, enlightening one another, and always speaking of Me, they are content and delighted.

Commentary: Krishna describes the joy of devotees immersed in his divine presence, encouraging communal devotion.

Shloka 10.10

तेषां सततयुक्तानां भजतां प्रीतिपूर्वकम्।
ददामि बुद्धियोगं तं येन मां उपयान्ति ते।।

उन सदा युक्त और प्रेमपूर्वक भजने वालों को मैं बुद्धियोग देता हूँ, जिससे वे मुझे प्राप्त करते हैं।

To those who are constantly devoted and worship Me with love, I give the Yoga of understanding, by which they come to Me.

Commentary: Krishna’s promise of divine wisdom to devotees fosters trust in the path of Bhakti.

Shloka 10.11

तेषामेवानुकम्पार्थमहमज्ञानजं तमः।
नाशयाम्यात्मभावस्थो ज्ञानदीपेन भास्वता।।

उन पर अनुग्रह करने के लिए मैं उनके हृदय में स्थित होकर अज्ञानजन्य अंधकार को ज्ञान के दीपक से नष्ट करता हूँ।

Out of compassion for them, I, dwelling in their hearts, destroy the darkness born of ignorance with the radiant lamp of knowledge.

Commentary: Krishna’s grace dispels ignorance, reinforcing the power of divine guidance in spiritual growth.

Shloka 10.20

अहमात्मा गुडाकेश सर्वं भूताशयस्थितः।
अहमादिश्च मध्यं च भूतानामन्त एव च।।

हे गुडाकेश! मैं सभी प्राणियों के हृदय में स्थित आत्मा हूँ। मैं प्राणियों का आदि, मध्य और अंत हूँ।

O Gudakesha, I am the Self seated in the hearts of all beings. I am the beginning, middle, and end of all beings.

Commentary: Krishna’s presence as the inner Self inspires devotees to see the divine within all.

Shloka 10.21

आदित्यानामहं विष्णुर्ज्योतिषां रविरंशुमान्।
मरीचिर्मरुतामस्मि नक्षत्राणामहं शशी।।

आदित्यों में मैं विष्णु, ज्योतियों में सूर्य, मरुतों में मरीचि, और नक्षत्रों में चंद्रमा हूँ।

Among the Adityas, I am Vishnu; among luminaries, the radiant sun; among the Maruts, I am Marichi; among the stars, I am the moon.

Commentary: Krishna begins listing his divine glories, inspiring awe by manifesting in creation’s finest aspects.

Shloka 10.22

वेदानां सामवेदोऽस्मि देवानामस्मि वासवः।
इन्द्रियाणां मनश्चास्मि भूतानामस्मि चेतना।।

वेदों में मैं सामवेद, देवताओं में वासव, इन्द्रियों में मन, और प्राणियों में चेतना हूँ।

Among the Vedas, I am the Sama Veda; among the gods, I am Vasava; among the senses, I am the mind; among beings, I am consciousness.

Commentary: Krishna’s presence in sacred texts and consciousness deepens devotees’ spiritual perception.

Shloka 10.23

रुद्राणां शङ्करश्चास्मि वित्तेशो यक्षरक्षसाम्।
वसूनां पावकश्चास्मि मेरुः शिखरिणामहम्।।

रुद्रों में मैं शंकर, यक्ष-राक्षसों में वित्तेश, वसुओं में पावक, और पर्वतों में मेरु हूँ।

Among the Rudras, I am Shankara; among Yakshas and Rakshasas, I am Kubera; among the Vasus, I am Pavaka; among mountains, I am Meru.

Commentary: Krishna’s manifestations in divine and natural forms encourage seeing the divine everywhere.

Shloka 10.24

पुरोधसां च मुख्यं मां विद्धि पार्थ बृहस्पतिम्।
सेनानीनामहं स्कन्दः सरसामस्मि सागरः।।

हे पार्थ! पुरोहितों में मुझे बृहस्पति, सेनापतियों में स्कंद, और सरोवरों में सागर जान।

O Partha, among priests, know Me as Brihaspati; among generals, I am Skanda; among bodies of water, I am the ocean.

Commentary: Krishna’s presence in leadership and nature’s vastness inspires reverence for his divine manifestations.

Shloka 10.25

महर्षीणां भृगुरहं गिरामस्म्येकमक्षरम्।
यज्ञानां जपयज्ञोऽस्मि स्थावराणां हिमालयः।।

महर्षियों में मैं भृगु, वाणी में एकाक्षर (ॐ), यज्ञों में जपयज्ञ, और स्थावरों में हिमालय हूँ।

Among great sages, I am Bhrigu; among words, I am the single syllable (Om); among sacrifices, I am the chanting sacrifice; among immovable things, I am the Himalaya.

Commentary: Krishna’s identification with sacred sound and nature’s grandeur deepens spiritual awareness.

Shloka 10.26

अश्वत्थः सर्ववृक्षाणां देवर्षीणां च नारदः।
गन्धर्वाणां चित्ररथः सिद्धानां कपिलो मुनिः।।

सभी वृक्षों में मैं अश्वत्थ, देवर्षियों में नारद, गंधर्वों में चित्ररथ, और सिद्धों में कपिल मुनि हूँ।

Among all trees, I am the Ashvattha; among divine sages, I am Narada; among Gandharvas, I am Chitraratha; among the perfected, I am Kapila Muni.

Commentary: Krishna’s manifestations in sacred and celestial beings inspire devotion through divine association.

Shloka 10.30

प्रह्लादश्चास्मि दैत्यानां कालः कलयतामहम्।
मृगाणां च मृगेन्द्रोऽहं वैनतेयश्च पक्षिणाम्।।

दैत्यों में मैं प्रह्लाद, गणना करने वालों में काल, पशुओं में मृगराज (सिंह), और पक्षियों में वैनतेय (गरुड़) हूँ।

Among Daityas, I am Prahlada; among subduers, I am time; among beasts, I am the lion; among birds, I am Garuda.

Commentary: Krishna’s presence in time and powerful beings underscores his universal dominance and grace.

Shloka 10.32

सर्गाणामादिरन्तश्च मध्यं चैवाहमर्जुन।
अध्यात्मविद्या विद्यानां वादः प्रवदतामहम्।।

हे अर्जुन! सृष्टियों में मैं आदि, अंत और मध्य हूँ। विद्याओं में अध्यात्म विद्या, और वाद करने वालों में मैं वाद हूँ।

O Arjuna, among creations, I am the beginning, end, and middle; among sciences, I am the science of the self; among debaters, I am logic.

Commentary: Krishna’s role in creation and knowledge elevates spiritual wisdom as the highest pursuit.

Shloka 10.41

यद्यद्विभूतिमत्सत्त्वं श्रीमदूर्जितमेव वा।
तत्तदेवावगच्छ त्वं मम तेजोऽंशसंभवम्।।

जो कुछ भी विभूतिमान, श्रीमान या ऊर्जित है, उसे मेरे तेज के अंश से उत्पन्न जान।

Whatever is glorious, prosperous, or powerful, know that to be a manifestation of a fraction of My splendor.

Commentary: Krishna’s universal presence in all excellence inspires devotees to see the divine in everything.
